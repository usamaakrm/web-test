What is unclaimed assets?

Unclaimed assets are financial holdings or property that have not been collected by their rightful owners for an extended period. These assets can include bank accounts, insurance policies, retirement funds, stocks, utility deposits, inheritances, and more. Typically, assets become unclaimed because owners forget about them, pass away without clear instructions, or institutions have outdated contact information. Unclaimed assets are held by state governments, financial institutions, or other entities until rightful owners or heirs claim them. To reclaim unclaimed assets, individuals must identify and prove ownership through documentation and file a claim with the relevant institution or agency. Regularly checking for unclaimed assets is important to ensure they are not lost indefinitely.
